How will the data for your digital twin be captured?
Helpful Information | External Links
Digital twins use various sensors to capture real-time data. Temperature sensors monitor heat, pressure sensors measure liquids and gases, and humidity sensors track moisture levels. Position and speed sensors monitor moving parts and velocities, while motion sensors detect vibrations. Force sensors measure weights, and flow sensors gauge fluid rates. Light sensors control lighting, acoustic sensors monitor sounds, and gas sensors detect air quality. Optical sensors, like cameras and LIDAR, handle imaging and distances. Sensor choice depends on the application, ensuring precise data for accurate modeling. An analysis of the most commonly used sensors in IoT devices can be found in (Montalvo et al., 2022).
